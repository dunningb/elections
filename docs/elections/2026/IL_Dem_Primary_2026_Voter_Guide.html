🗳 Illinois Democratic Primary — March 17, 2026

Northfield Township · Cook County Specimen Ballot · Candidate Comparison Guide

United States Senator
Open seat — replacing retiring Sen. Dick Durbin. 10 Democrats on the primary ballot. Three frontrunners dominate polling and fundraising. Winner is heavily favored in November in deep-blue Illinois.
⚠️ March polling: Krishnamoorthi ~35–39%, Stratton ~28–38%, Kelly ~12–18%. The remaining 7 candidates are polling at 3% combined or less.
Raja Krishnamoorthi FRONTRUNNER
U.S. Rep., IL-8 (Schaumburg) · 5 terms

Led in polling and fundraising throughout the race, raising $30+ million. Known for House Oversight work and as a China hawk on the select committee on U.S.-China competition. Background in engineering, law, and small business.

Immigration: Wants to "abolish Trump's ICE" and revamp DHS/CBP. Under fire for donations from Palantir's CTO (an ICE contractor); campaign donated equivalent amount to charity.

Healthcare: Expand Medicaid and a public option. Not Medicare for All. Opposes GOP Medicaid cuts.

Economy: "American Dream" platform — homeowner tax credit, free school lunches, raise federal minimum wage.

Key endorsements: Local suburban officials; AIPAC endorsed in prior congressional races.

Moderate-progressive Top fundraiser $30M+ Public option Reform ICE
🔗 krishnamoorthi.com
Juliana Stratton
Illinois Lt. Governor (Chicago) · 2 terms

First to enter the race. Gained momentum in final weeks, closing the gap with Krishnamoorthi. Backed heavily by Gov. Pritzker and Sen. Duckworth. Positioned as the most progressive of the top three.

Immigration: The only top-3 candidate calling to abolish ICE outright. Attacked Krishnamoorthi repeatedly for donor ties to ICE contractors.

Healthcare: Supports stronger public coverage options; opposes for-profit healthcare structures.

Key endorsements: Gov. JB Pritzker, Sen. Tammy Duckworth, suburban House Democrats, Sen. Elizabeth Warren (rallied for her).

Progressive Pritzker + Duckworth endorsed Abolish ICE
🔗 julianastratton.com
Robin Kelly
U.S. Rep., IL-2 (Lynwood) · 7 terms

Seven-term congresswoman; former IL Democratic Party chair. Running on "People Over Profits" — casting herself as the experienced, policy-focused alternative to the Krishnamoorthi-Stratton clash.

Immigration: Wants to "dismantle" ICE. The most progressive on Gaza: only candidate to sign the Block the Bombs bill, call situation a genocide, and pledge no AIPAC money.

Key endorsements: Congressional Black Caucus, gun violence prevention orgs, LGBTQ+ groups.

Progressive Anti-AIPAC pledge Dismantle ICE Trailing in polls
🔗 robinkelly.com

North suburban district covering Lake and Cook counties (Highland Park, Northbrook, Wheeling, Glenview, Northfield, etc.). Six-term incumbent faces a progressive challenger from the left.
Brad Schneider INCUMBENT
U.S. Rep., IL-10 (Highland Park) · 6 terms · age 64

Northwestern/Kellogg MBA. Senior member of House Ways & Means Committee; chairs the 110-member New Democrat Coalition. First elected 2012. Background in business consulting.

Immigration: Reform ICE with strict new standards — body cameras, mandatory ID display, no masks during arrests, use-of-force standards. Opposed abolishing agency; pushed for impeachment inquiry of DHS Sec. Noem.

Healthcare: Defend and expand ACA; opposes for-profit barriers to access. Not Medicare for All.

Iran: Voted for (unsuccessful) resolution to end military operation; opposed continued funding.

Endorsed by: Gov. Pritzker, Sen. Duckworth, Chicago Tribune editorial board.

Moderate Reform ICE ACA defender Pritzker + Duckworth endorsed

Cook County is the 2nd largest county in the U.S. The Board President oversees a ~$10 billion annual budget, 22,000+ employees, the county jail, courts, the public health system, and 70,000+ acres of Forest Preserves.
Toni Preckwinkle INCUMBENT
Cook County Board President (2010–present) · age 79

Seeking a fifth term — which would tie the record for longest tenure in the office. Former South Side alderperson (4th Ward) and high school history teacher. Also chairs the Cook County Democratic Party.

Record: 15 years of balanced budgets with no county property tax hikes. Erased $600M+ in medical debt. Helped eliminate cash bail in Illinois. Championed guaranteed income programs. County pension fund at 66% funded — better than Chicago's.

Attacks on Reilly: Ties to Trump Tower (donations); voted against expanding Chicago's sanctuary city ordinance in 2021.

Progressive No property tax hikes in 15 yrs Balanced budgets Cash bail elimination
🔗 tonipreckwinkle.org
Brendan Reilly CHALLENGER
Chicago Ald., 42nd Ward (Downtown) · ~19 years · age 54

Represents the Loop, River North, Streeterville, and Magnificent Mile. Considered Preckwinkle's toughest challenger yet. Running as a pro-business, affordability-focused "change" candidate.

Key attack: A decade-long property tax technology overhaul that cost $120M+ and caused months of late bills in 2025. Also criticizes Preckwinkle's support for ex-State's Atty Kim Foxx and the county's electronic monitoring failures.

Defense: Donated Trump campaign contributions to charity; stood alongside Preckwinkle at anti-Trump "No Kings" rally.
